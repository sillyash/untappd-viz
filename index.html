Does brewery type affect the number of different beers produced ?

This graph shows the average number of beers produced by brewery type.

We can see that the average number of beers produced by a "Nano Brewery", is about 11.69, far more than the second highest, which are "Brew Pubs", with about 7.57 different beers made.

This can be explained by the fact that "Nano Breweries" are usually very small and independent, and thus have more freedom to experiment and create new beers, while "Brew Pubs" are usually more focused on serving a specific clientele and thus have a more limited range of beers.

The other brewery types have a much lower average number of beers produced, with "Micro Breweries" at 6.56, "Contract Breweries" at 5.28, and "Macro Breweries" (large breweries) at 2.92.

This shows that the size and type of brewery does have an impact on the number of different beers produced, with smaller and more independent breweries being able to produce a wider variety of beers, far ahead of larger breweries, for the exception of "Home Breweries".

Also, it is interesting to note that the number of different beers produced is measured across a span of only 2017 to 2020, which is a relatively short period of time, increasing the gap between brewery types.

Average note for beers per type

This graphs now shows the average note of beer types.

We see here a combination of what we learned before with the other graphs.
On the first hand we mesure a repartition similar to chekins count (in a reduced scale).
And, on the other hand, we see a normalization happening between 3 and 4.

Now, it is interesting to note the differences between the average notes and the chekins count.
If you look closely, you an see a general tendency for higher ranked beer to have less chekins while, beers with a lot of checkins tend to have more mixed opinions.

It's very interesting to see that over a larger number of notations, the rating semms to be a lot more balanced and we mesure a trend to less extrem ratings.

This form of graph was a recurring element in our study and although we see it in different scalling, the ratio of notes and cheskins looks like everywhere and whatever the object is.

Practical exemple : Alivaria Beloe Zoloto

here we can see all the ratings given to the beer Alivaria Beloe Zoloto
